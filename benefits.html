BENEFITS

HEALTH AND FITNESS BENEFITS


1. Improves cardiovascular health and lowers blood pressure

Muay Thai involves a lot of high intensity interval training (HIIT), in which one alternates between periods of vigorous movement and lower intensity exercises. This method of training has been shown to be one of the most effective and rapid techniques to developing and strengthening the heart muscles. This lowers blood pressure and greatly reduces the risk of various heart diseases like coronary artery disease, heart failure and strokes.


2. Lowers cholesterol

Muay Thai exercises stimulate enzymes that transfer low-density lipoproteins (LDL) from your blood vessels to your liver, which are then removed as waste. This contributes to lowering your bad cholesterol levels and losing weight.


3. Regulates glucose and insulin levels

High levels of physical exercise aids insulin with the transport of glucose to cells to be used for respiration. This controls both insulin and glucose levels, reducing systemic inflammation and the risk of diabetes.

4. Burns body fat

HIIT training is one of the most effective ways of burning the stubborn subcutaneous fat just under your skin. This burn not only occurs during workouts, but lasts for long after it as well, due to the increased release of growth hormones.


5. Develops stamina

Continuous and regular HIIT training is also a method to building stamina quickly, due to constant movement during both high and low periods of exercise intensity.


6. Increases flexibility

Various movements like kicks and knees automatically aid in increasing your flexibility. Each time the movement is carried out, your muscles and joints stretch towards their maximum movement range.


7. Enhances balance

During training, sparring and drills will require precision and speed, with balance to bring the two aspects together. Whether it be nailing that landing after a powerful kick, or leaning backwards to dodge a blow, balance is a key skill.

MENTAL BENEFITS


1. Develops self confidence

Muay Thai constantly challenges your body and mind, forcing you to step out of your comfort zone and acknowledge your strengths. As your fitness levels rise, you not only look better but feel better as well. The knowledge of your abilities to defend yourself and carry out difficult physical tasks is a great boost to your confidence.


2. Improves mental focus

Muay Thai involves intense focus on whatever movements and techniques you are carrying out, due to the high physical and mental requirements needed for this martial art. This focus carries on to your everyday life to whatever task is at hand.

3. Provides a stress outlet

Angry at that group member that makes you do all the work, or that annoying coworker? We’ve got you! Punching bags and pads allow you to vent your frustration out with no complaints. Also, as mentioned above, the focus needed in Muay Thai doesn’t allow you to think of anything else outside the boxing ring, giving your mind a rest from daily worries.


4. Induces self discipline

Techniques require patience and practice to develop correctly in a structured, repetitive environment. This conditions you to listen to instructions well and push your body to the max during training. With great knowledge also comes great responsibility. You will have to take responsibility for the self-defense skills you learn in Muay Thai, knowing when and where to use it.
